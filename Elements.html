Jellyfish

Jellyfish (also known as sea jellies) are ancient, gelatinous invertebrates that have inhabited the world's oceans for over 500 million years, predating the dinosaurs. Despite their name, they are not fish but members of the phylum Cnidaria, closely related to corals and sea anemones.
